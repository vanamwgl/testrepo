Who We Are

A Registered Non-Profit Committed to Human Dignity

Indian Social Service NGO is a registered Non-Profit, Non-Governmental social service organization implementing projects for community development in Warangal District, Telangana.

We concentrate on Education, Health, Environment, Women and Child Development, and Skill Development — providing trainings, placements, and support for rural and urban unemployed youth and their families.

Our flagship Swadhar Greh / Shakti Sadan shelter home, supported by the Ministry of Women and Child Development, Government of India, serves as a safe haven for women and girls in distress.

Shakti Sadan – Shelter Home

  • Centrally located in Kashibugga, near MGM Hospital, Police Station, Mahila Police Station, District Court and CWC
  • Provides shelter, food, clothing, health care, and social security to distressed women and girls
  • Admits residents through Sakhi Center, Police Department, Child Welfare Committee, DCPU, and Legal Services Authority
  • Provides vocational training (Tailoring, Computer) and psychosocial counseling
  • Works with cases of domestic violence, POCSO, child marriages, trafficking, and more
  • Facilitates reunion, rehabilitation, and reintegration into society

Success Story

Reuniting Mangamma with Her Family

"

A woman was found in a helpless condition in Kapulakanparthy village. Local residents informed Sangam Police Station, who tried to trace her identity — but could not, because she was deaf and mute. She was admitted to Swadhar Greh, Kashibugga through the Sakhi Center on instruction of the District Women Officer.

The team tried tracing her through media, newspapers, and WhatsApp networks — without success. They recognized she was receiving a pension from the Telangana Government, and approached the Aadhaar Center. With the cooperation of Sri Pradeep Kumar (Aadhaar Center Incharge), DWO, and Field Response Officer Sri Anirudh, they identified her details through the "Missing and Re-union Aadhaar Services."

Her name was Mangamma, from Laxmipuram village, Kodad Mandal, Suryapet District, Telangana. On 13 March 2023, she was reunited with her family before the District Collector in the Grievance Cell, Collector Office, Warangal.

Outcome: Successfully reunited with family · Coordinated across DWO, UIDAI, Aadhaar Center, Women & Child Department, and Indian Social Service NGO. Recognized as the best success story of Swadhar, Kashibugga, Warangal.
