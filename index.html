About ISS

Indian Social Service works for community development with a focus on women and child welfare, education, health awareness, environmental action, and skill development for unemployed youth.

Institutional profile

The organization presents itself as a registered social service institution working with support from state agencies, central government schemes, and community partnerships. Its public profile is strongest when framed around service delivery, rehabilitation, awareness building, and livelihood support.

Programs

Key programs and recurring themes drawn from the annual reports.

Women & Girls

Swadhar Greh

Shelter, counseling, rehabilitation support, and vocational opportunities for distressed women and girls.

Skills

PMKVY Training

Industry-relevant youth training, soft skills, spoken English, and placement-linked support.

Livelihoods

MEPMA / DAY-NULM

Urban livelihood and placement-oriented training for unemployed youth in municipal areas.

Children

Child Rights

Awareness around survival, protection, development, and participation for school-age children.

Justice

Legal Awareness

Programs covering women’s rights, child rights, marriage registration, domestic violence, and legal access.

Health & Environment

Awareness Campaigns

Community outreach on HIV/AIDS, TB, cancer, substance abuse, sanitation, and environmental responsibility.

Impact themes

The documents show a consistent mix of institutional care, skills training, awareness building, and community mobilization.

Rehabilitation

Support for women and girls through shelter, referrals, counseling, and reintegration pathways.

Employability

Training youth for retail, medical sales, solar-related work, and other livelihood-linked opportunities.

Community awareness

Campaigns on rights, health, sanitation, anti-addiction, and environmental practices in urban and rural areas.
